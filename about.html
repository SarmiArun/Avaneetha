About Us

Avaneetha Textiles (P) Ltd.,

One of the best yarn manufacturers in India.India's Avaneetha Textiles (P) Ltd. is one of the largest yarn suppliers to the knitting and weaving industries.

Our goals

Founded in 2004, due to the vision of a group of managing partners who desire to build a high-quality yarn manufacturing company, Avaneetha Textiles is headquartered in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu, India. The company has a solid technical force and advanced construction equipment with first-class technical personnel and a modern management level. As the company developed famous brand products with the same level of advanced industry technology over the years, it closely followed the advanced international production and textile technology.

2006:
Our initial spindle capacity was 15,600.

2008:
A total of 20,400 spindles are being added to the company.

2015:
Expanded to 24,000 spindles. A total of 60000 spindles are now available

Quality

Continual innovation through continuous research and development makes us strong in quality.
A full-fledged quality control department tests the quality of raw materials and finished goods for each division.
Various stringent tests are conducted on every product we manufacture or procure to ensure quality and consistency.
Our team of qualified professionals conducts extensive research to produce superior quality fabrics.
Avaneetha Textiles constantly innovates new ideas to enhance our product portfolio through state-of-the-art technology, manufacturing, supply, and technical facilities.

The textile industry is committed to excellence in every aspect.

Each year, we strive to reach new horizons and grow continuously.

Having an experienced management team provides us with strength and direction.

Having a consistent growth while maintaining a balance between profitability and social welfare.

Our company values the communities in which it operates.

Starting with its facility in Coimbatore, Avaneetha, since its inception, has grown into one of the largest producers of 100% cotton yarns in India.
